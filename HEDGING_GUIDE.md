## ðŸŽ­ **Five Hedging Strategies**

Our system uses **5 different strategies**, each targeting specific linguistic patterns:

### **Strategy 1: Modal Verb Replacement (30%)**

**Purpose:** Replace strong, absolute verbs with hedged versions

**Replacements:**
| Strong Verb | Hedged Alternatives |
|-------------|---------------------|
| proves | suggests, indicates, may prove, appears to prove |
| demonstrates | suggests, indicates, seems to demonstrate |
| shows | suggests, indicates, appears to show, tends to show |
| confirms | supports, suggests, may confirm |
| guarantees | may ensure, likely ensures, tends to ensure |
| ensures | may ensure, helps ensure, can ensure |
| is/are | may be, could be, might be, appears to be |
| will | may, might, could, would likely |
| causes | may cause, can cause, tends to cause |
| solves | may solve, can help solve, tends to solve |

**Examples:**

```
Before: "AI proves that automation is effective."
After: "AI suggests that automation is effective."

Before: "Data shows clear trends."
After: "Data appears to show clear trends."

After: "AI might transform industries."

Before: "This guarantees success."
After: "This may ensure success."
```

**Purpose:** Add epistemic markers to express uncertainty about knowledge
